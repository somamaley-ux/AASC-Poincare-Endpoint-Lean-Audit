# Weakening-Resistance Audit

This audit records the hardening pass responding to the same-mode question whether a weaker local kernel packet could preserve non-degenerate same-carrier endpoint use while permitting negative endpoint governance.

## Added manuscript section

- Section: `Weakening-Resistance of the Local Kernel Packet`
- Primary theorem: `No strict same-carrier weakening permits independent negative governance`
- Primary corollary: `Strict weakening either preserves the packet extensionally or exits endpoint use`

## Audited nodes

| Node | Local role | Weakening failure |
|---|---|---|
| K5 | Unique admissible interior | Plural endpoint-governing interiors collapse to bookkeeping, domain split, or second classifier. |
| K6 | Standing-admissibility identity | Endpoint standing outside admissibility is support-only, carrier shift, or second status gate. |
| K11 | UEAP report preservation | Over-reports become unsupported endpoint authority, silent redescription, or no-carrier transfer. |
| K13 | Endpoint exhaustion | Unknown, deferred, unsupported, silent, bookkeeping, and hidden-fifth statuses are report/epistemic statuses, not endpoint truth branches. |

## Claim boundary

The audit distinguishes minimal report evaluability from K11 no-overreporting, and target-slot fixation from K13 endpoint exhaustion. K11 and K13 are treated as local closure theorems, not as assumptions hidden inside the definition of a weakening.

The manuscript does not claim that every possible formalism must use exactly the same AASC vocabulary. It claims the narrower local necessity result: for the fixed Poincare endpoint under theorem-bearing same-carrier use, weakening K5, K6, K11, or K13 either becomes extensionally equivalent for endpoint purposes or exits non-degenerate endpoint use.
